Latest From Our Blog

Senior Women Citizens for Change (SWCC) is a local non-partisan and non-profit making organization committed to promotion and protection of the rights of the older women who serve as care givers for orphaned and vulnerable children. SWCC does this through sensitization and awareness raising, counseling, monitoring and documentation of violations of older women rights that are significantly violated by members of the society. The Mission of SWCC is to provide a community platform for community to discuss challenges facing ageing so as to promote, protect and enhance the rights of elderly women through improvement of the status of elderly women. SWCC received funding from OSIEA to run for three years (36 months) with a proposed start date January 2018. The project was implemented in Butere, Ikolomani and Mumias of Kakamega County in Western part of Kenya targeted at reaching out to 3000 Community Members (2000 older persons &1000 youth). The project is anchored under Open Society institute in East Africa (OSIEA) Economic and Social Empowerment program supporting the Older Persons and the Youth. In 2023, March SWCC also ran a monitoring and evaluation exercise in Kakamega county ( Butere and Eregi).The main focus of this project was to build the capacity of the older persons and youth in bridging the gap of social inequality. Considering the fact that the older women are weak relative to the youth the youth shall be enjoined in the project to bring synergy so as to ensure the older also enjoy programs that empower the youth in a ripple effect. This was a project that is unique in its design in that it brings together groups that are in the extremes in terms of strength and experience, these two will play a complementary role. The project is all rounded for it endeavor's to build the knowledge base of the beneficiaries and empower them to know their rights. The project also intends to support the older market their produce.
